Project Introduction

Budget Buddy is a budgeting and expense management platform tailored to college students and their common financial hardships and goals.

The platform is designed to not only address the monetary challenges of managing rent, groceries, tuition, and social spending but the emotional stress of it as well.

Participants

Study Group

  • 6 college students, aged 20–22
  • Mix of:
    • 2 Advanced Budgeters
      • Use data-heavy spreadsheets
    • 2 Informal Budgeters
      • Rely on mental budgeting
    • 2 Non-Budgeters
      • At most check bank balance occasionally

What we found:

  • Emotional stress drives avoidance of structured systems
  • Manual tracking feels tedious
  • Motivation to use a product decreases without visible progress
  • Users want financial clarity but not added complexity and work
  • Users check their balance often, but do not track spending intentionally

Storyboard: Three Levels of Tasks

Level 1: Simple Task

This task uses notifications and widgets to remind the user to log their spending. This simple task encourages user retention and healthy budgeting practices.

Level 2: Medium Task

Describe the intermediate interaction scenario.

Level 3: Complex Task

This task uses a cute character to show a visual representation of your budgeting skills. It shows a dashboard of your streak, financial health, nav bar, log expense button, and shop or help button. If you don't maintain healthy spending habits or streaks your budget buddy could get mad or even die! This encourages younger people like students to see budgeting as something fun like a game.
